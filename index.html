Why do you need a personal injury attorney after an accident?

If you’ve been injured in an accident, you must get legal help as soon as possible. A personal injury attorney can help you file a claim and get your compensation. Here are some reasons why you need a personal injury attorney after an accident:

You may be entitled to compensation.


After an accident, you may be entitled to compensation for your medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering. A personal injury attorney will fight for your rights and ensure you get the total compensation you deserve.

You must prove that the other party was at fault to win your injury case. This can be done by gathering evidence such as witness statements, photos, and medical records. Your attorney will also help build your case by investigating the accident and interviewing witnesses.

Once you have gathered the evidence, your attorney will file a personal injury lawsuit on your behalf. The case will seek compensation for your damages, including medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering. If the other party is found to be at fault, they will be required to pay your damages.

An insurance company is not on your side.


After an accident, the insurance company will try to minimize the money they have to pay. They may do this by offering you a low settlement or denying your claim altogether. A personal injury attorney will protect your interests and ensure you get the fair treatment you deserve from the insurance company.

If you’ve been in an accident, the insurance company may try to lowball you on your settlement or deny your claim. A personal injury attorney will protect your interests and ensure you get the fair treatment you deserve from the insurer.

The statute of limitations may apply.


There is a time limit for filing a personal injury claim, known as the statute of limitations. If you wait too long to file your claim, you may be barred from getting any compensation at all. A personal injury attorney will ensure your claim is filed on time so you don’t miss out on the compensation you deserve.

Most personal injury claims are governed by a statute of limitations, which is a law that sets a time limit on how long you have to file a claim. In most states, the statute of limitations for personal injury claims is two years from the date of the accident. If you were injured in an accident on January 1, 2020, you would have until January 1, 2022, to file your claim. If you wait for longer, you will likely be barred from getting any compensation.

There are some exceptions to the general two-year statute of limitations. For example, if the government employed the person who caused your injuries, you may have a shorter or longer period to file your claim, depending on the state. Additionally, if you were injured as a minor, you may have more time to file your claim.

A personal injury attorney will know the statute of limitations for your particular claim and ensure that your claim is filed on time. If you try to handle your claim, you may miss the deadline and be barred from getting any compensation. Your attorney will file the necessary paperwork and represent you in court, ensuring your rights are protected every step. of the way.

You have enough to worry about after an accident without navigating the legal system alone. A personal injury attorney will take care of everything for you to focus on your recovery.
